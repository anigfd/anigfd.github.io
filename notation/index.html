PV inversion & staircases (barotropic, ff- or β\beta-plane)

SymbolMeaningConvention
q=ζ+βyq=\zeta+\beta y(absolute) potential vorticitymaterial conservation: qt+J(ψ,q)=0q_t+J(\psi,q)=0

The invertibility principle. Given q(x,y)q(x,y) everywhere, a balance condition (ζ=∇2ψ\zeta=\nabla^2\psi, i.e. nondivergent flow), and boundary conditions (here: periodic), ψ\psi — and hence u\mathbf u — is uniquely determined: psi_hat = grid.invert_laplacian(zeta_hat) with ζ=q−βy\zeta=q-\beta y. gfdlib.pv.gaussian_blob places a periodic-safe PV anomaly at a point; gfdlib.pv.staircase_pv builds an idealized alternating-band PV staircase (a smoothed square wave in yy). Inverting a staircase shows the jet cores (uu extrema) sit exactly at the sharp PV risers between bands, not within the well-mixed plateaus — verified numerically (local ∣qy∣|q_y| at each jet core is ∼\sim20×\times the domain-mean ∣qy∣|q_y|).

Both growth-rate problems are solved as small eigenvalue problems derived here, not copied from a closed-form formula. gfdlib. baroclinic.eady_growth_rate matches the boundary-value problem’s interior solution to the linearized thermal boundary condition at z=0,Hz=0,H; verified against the published benchmark (μmax≈1.61\mu_{max}\approx1.61, σmax≈0.31\sigma_{max}\approx0.31, cutoff μc≈2.399\mu_c\approx2.399; Vallis/Pedlosky) to within grid resolution. gfdlib.baroclinic.invert_2layer is the 2-layer QGPV Helmholtz solve (closed-form 2×22\times2 inverse); rhs_2layer is spectral-space, for use as the nonlinear RHS in gfdlib.timestep.ifrk4_step with hyperviscosity as the (scalar, both-layers-identical) linear operator — the mean-flow and β\beta terms stay in the nonlinear part since they couple the two layers (2×22\times2 per wavenumber, not a scalar). Cross-checked against an independently-derived linear eigenvalue: a small-amplitude single-wavenumber perturbation’s measured growth rate converges to the predicted value to <0.1%<0.1\% once the mode purifies (a coincidental choice of parameters that placed an integer wavenumber exactly at the marginal-stability cutoff was caught and avoided during this check).

A fixed mean shear with no drag is an unlimited energy source. Nonlinearly, rhs_2layer without Ekman drag (r=0r=0) was found to grow without bound regardless of how much hyperviscosity was added — energy conservation was verified exact for the pure nonlinear (Jacobian) terms in isolation, so the runaway traced to the mean-flow/β\beta terms having no saturating mechanism, not a sign error. Adding bottom drag on the lower layer’s relative vorticity (r>0, the standard ingredient in Phillips’ original 1954 model) produces the expected life cycle — exponential growth, a peak, decay, and equilibration — confirmed by a multi-hundred-time-unit run at the notebook’s actual resolution.

Barotropic/baroclinic mode decomposition (ch. 10). ψbt=(ψ1+ψ2)/2\psi_{bt}=(\psi_1+\psi_2)/2, ψbc=(ψ1−ψ2)/2\psi_{bc}=(\psi_1-\psi_2)/2 decouple the two-layer PV equations EXACTLY: summing/differencing q1=∇2ψ1+F(ψ2−ψ1)q_1=\nabla^2\psi_1+F(\psi_2-\psi_1), q2=∇2ψ2+F(ψ1−ψ2)q_2=\nabla^2\psi_2+F(\psi_1-\psi_2) cancels the FF terms in the sum and doubles them in the difference, giving qbt=∇2ψbtq_{bt}=\nabla^2\psi_{bt} (exactly ch. 7/18’s barotropic PV, no FF at all) and qbc=∇2ψbc−2Fψbcq_{bc}=\nabla^2\psi_{bc}-2F\psi_{bc} (exactly ch. 8’s single-layer QGPV, with deformation parameter 2F2F). gfdlib.baroclinic. to_modes/from_modes implement the (trivial, linear) change of variables; pv_2layer is the forward operator (exact inverse of invert_2layer). Verified directly, not just asserted from the algebra: to_modes applied to pv_2layer’s output matches the two decoupled single-layer operators to 10−810^{-8}. The decoupling holds only for the LINEAR operator — the Jacobian nonlinearity in rhs_2layer still couples the two modes, which is exactly what ch. 10’s notebook demonstrates (a disturbance seeded purely in qbcq_{bc} measurably leaks barotropic KE through nonlinear self-advection alone).

Symmetric & inertial instability: one Ertel-PV sign criterion. For a zonal thermal-wind front, gfdlib.symmetric derives (not looks up) the Ertel PV q=(f−∂U/∂y)N2−f(∂U/∂z)2q=(f-\partial U/\partial y)N^2-f(\partial U/\partial z)^2 and shows the front is unstable to slantwise (symmetric) displacements iff qf<0qf<0, i.e. (1+Ro)<1/Ri(1+\mathrm{Ro})<1/\mathrm{Ri}. Two independently-checkable limits confirm the sign convention: at Ro=0\mathrm{Ro}=0 the marginal curve reduces to the textbook value Ri=1\mathrm{Ri}=1; for (1+Ro)<0(1+\mathrm{Ro})<0 (absolute vorticity changes sign) the front is unconditionally unstable at every Ri>0\mathrm{Ri}>0 — pure inertial instability, no stratification dependence. gfdlib.symmetric.classify labels any (Ro,>Ri)(\mathrm{Ro}, > \mathrm{Ri}) point stable / symmetric / inertial / gravitational (Ri<0\mathrm{Ri}<0) for the Ri-Ro stability map.

Kelvin-Helmholtz: the Taylor-Goldstein equation, and a real numerical pitfall caught along the way. gfdlib.instability. taylor_goldstein_growth_rate solves (U−c)2(ϕ′′−k2ϕ)−U′′(U−c)>ϕ+N2ϕ=0(U-c)^2(\phi''-k^2\phi)-U''(U-c) > \phi+N^2\phi=0 as a natively linear 2n×2n2n\times2n generalized eigenvalue problem (keeping the buoyancy perturbation as an independent unknown rather than eliminating it via division by (U−c)(U-c), which gives a mathematically equivalent but quadratic-in-cc pencil). At N2=0N^2=0 this reduces exactly (to 10−1310^{-13}) to gfdlib.instability.growth_rate — a strong internal cross-check, not an external one. For the classic Hazel (1972) profile, growth was initially found NOT to vanish cleanly above the rigorous Miles-Howard threshold Ri≥1/4\mathrm{Ri}\geq1/4 — a hard theorem violation is impossible, so this was root-caused, not dismissed: the spurious growth scaled with grid spacing Δz\Delta z alone (matched Δz\Delta z across different nn and domain sizes gave matched spurious magnitude) and shrank as Δz→0\Delta z\to0, confirming genuine 2nd-order truncation error — worst exactly at the delicate Ri=1/4\mathrm{Ri}=1/4 point, where the equation’s critical-layer indicial roots coalesce, a classically hard point for finite differences (professional codes use spectral methods here). Fixed by tightening the domain/resolution and applying a documented noise floor calibrated to this investigation (_TG_NOISE_FLOOR); the resulting curve decreases smoothly and reaches zero by J≈0.4J\approx0.4, correctly reproducing the theorem’s qualitative Ri≥1/4\geq1/4 cutoff (not a knife-edge at exactly 1/41/4, an honest limitation of a simple solver on this classically delicate problem). gfdlib.instability.hazel_profile builds U,N2U,N^2 for this test case.

A genuine bug to avoid re-introducing. With u=−ψz, w=ψxu=-\psi_z,\ w=\psi_x, vorticity is ζ=wx−uz=∇2ψ\zeta=w_x-u_z=\nabla^2\psi — the same sign as the horizontal streamfunction convention above, not ∇2ψ=−ζ\nabla^2\psi=-\zeta. Getting this backward flips the sign of the buoyancy-vorticity feedback and the instability never grows at any RaRa: verified numerically (see ch14 PR) that the wrong sign gives pure decay for all RaRa, while the correct sign reproduces subcritical decay (Ra<RacRa<Ra_c) and supercritical growth into finite-amplitude convection (Ra>RacRa>Ra_c) as expected. gfdlib.convection.ChannelGrid (periodic xx, Dirichlet zz) and rhs_boussinesq implement the corrected convention; lorenz_rhs is the (X,Y,Z)(X,Y,Z) truncation, sharing gfdlib.timestep.rk4 with every other chapter.

SymbolMeaningConvention

Munk’s (1966) abyssal recipe w ∂T/∂z=κ >∂2T/∂z2w\,\partial T/\partial z=\kappa\, > \partial^2T/\partial z^2 is solved exactly (gfdlib.overturning. abyssal_profile): a linear 2nd-order ODE with constant coefficients, general solution T=A+Bewz/κT=A+Be^{wz/\kappa}, with A,BA,B fixed by the two BCs via a 2×22\times2 numpy.linalg.solve. Large w/κw/\kappa sweeps the bottom value through most of the water column, compressing the transition to the surface value into a thin layer near z=Hz=H — the real ocean’s near-uniform abyssal temperature capped by a thin thermocline; verified numerically, not just plotted, against both this limit and the w→0w\to0 pure-diffusion (linear) profile.

The overturning cell reuses ch14’s Boussinesq machinery unchanged. gfdlib.overturning.rhs_overturning has the exact same structure as convection.rhs_boussinesq, but replaces the fixed background- stratification advection term (+ψx+\psi_x, uniform bottom heating) with an explicit, horizontally-varying heating field Q(x,z)Q(x,z) (gfdlib.overturning.surface_heating) — warm at one end of the (periodic) domain, cool at the other, concentrated near the surface. This is Rossby’s (1965) “horizontal convection” mechanism: differential heating along a boundary organizes into a single overturning cell (rising at the heated column, sinking at the cooled one) rather than the many small convective rolls of uniform bottom heating — verified directly, not just asserted: the vertical velocity w=ψxw=\psi_x comes out positive under the heated column and negative under the cooled one in every run tested.

Eddy transport & mixing (passive tracer in 2D turbulence)

SymbolMeaningConvention
Γ=dCˉ/dy\Gamma=d\bar C/dyimposed mean tracer gradientCtotal=Γy+c′(x,y,t)C_{total}=\Gamma y+c'(x,y,t), c′c' periodic

The mean-gradient trick. A passive tracer with an unbounded mean gradient is handled the same way ch16/ch18 handle a mean shear or βy\beta y: split into an imposed linear part and a periodic perturbation c′c', so gfdlib.mixing.rhs_coupled can evolve c′c' on the same doubly- periodic spectral.Grid as the vorticity that stirs it, with the mean gradient appearing as an ordinary source term −Γv-\Gamma v (the tracer is passive: it’s advected by, but does not feed back on, the vorticity).

KeffK_{eff} is intrinsically Γ\Gamma-independent — verified, not assumed. The c′c' equation is linear in c′c', so c′∝Γc'\propto\Gamma for a fixed flow realization and Keff=−v′c′‾/ΓK_{eff}=-\overline{v'c'}/\Gamma must be exactly independent of Γ\Gamma. Checked numerically: two runs on the identical vorticity history with Γ\Gamma differing by a factor of 2 give KeffK_{eff} agreeing to machine precision (relative difference 10−1610^{-16}), and a control run with the velocity field identically zero gives Keff=0K_{eff}=0 exactly (no possible eddy flux without a flow). For freely- decaying 2D turbulence, KeffK_{eff} comes out positive (down-gradient) and of the same order as a mixing-length estimate urms×u_{rms}\times(domain scale) — a real emergent result of the simulation, not tuned to match.

Wind-driven circulation (Stommel; Munk)

SymbolMeaningConvention
ε\varepsilonStommel nondimensional bottom-drag parameterboundary layer width ∼ε\sim\varepsilon
δ\deltaMunk nondimensional lateral-friction parameterboundary layer width ∼δ\sim\delta
f(x)f(x)west-east profile, ψ=f(x)sin⁡(πy)\psi=f(x)\sin(\pi y)f(0)=0f(0)=0 always; f′(0)=0f'(0)=0 too for Munk (no-slip)

A genuinely new domain shape. Every earlier chapter’s domain was doubly-periodic or a channel with two boundaries; this is the first bounded rectangular basin, four walls. gfdlib.circulation solves the Stommel and Munk gyre problems two independent ways: an exact analytic solution (separating ψ=f(x)sin⁡(πy)\psi=f(x)\sin(\pi y) reduces the PDE to a linear ODE for f(x)f(x) with constant coefficients, solved via its characteristic polynomial’s roots — quadratic formula for Stommel, numpy.roots for Munk’s quartic — and a small numpy.linalg.solve for the boundary constants), and a general 2D finite-difference direct solve (Kronecker- sum operators, numpy.linalg.solve, no SciPy) that works for arbitrary forcing, cross-validated against the analytic solution with clean 2nd-order convergence (gfdlib tests).

Munk’s biharmonic operator is handled by keeping ζ=∇2ψ\zeta=\nabla^2\psi as an independent field (the same pattern as ch16/17/19’s coupled states) rather than a hand-derived 4th-order stencil, closed with Thom’s (1933) wall-vorticity formula ζwall=2ψadjacent/d2\zeta_{wall}=2\psi_{adjacent}/d^2 (derived by Taylor-expanding ψ\psi from a ψ=ψn=0\psi=\psi_n=0 wall) for no-slip on the east/west walls, with free-slip (ζ=0\zeta=0, no correction needed) on the north/south walls — the standard textbook simplification that keeps the problem separable.

A real bug, caught by a convergence test, not an eyeball check. An earlier version enforced no-slip on all four walls (matching physical intuition, but not the separable-solution assumption). It disagreed with the exact analytic solution by a stubborn ∼30%\sim30\% — but critically, that error did not shrink with grid refinement at any boundary-layer width tested (0.08 to 0.4), immediately distinguishing a genuine discretization error (which must vanish as Δx→0\Delta x\to0) from a boundary-condition mismatch (which does not). The fix — free-slip on north/south, matching the classical Pedlosky/Vallis treatment — restored clean 2nd-order convergence.

A real, instructive bug: equal-weight ghost points broke matrix symmetry. A first implementation of gfdlib.stratification. vertical_modes used naive ghost-point differencing for the Neumann BCs; it gave eigenvalues that LOOKED nearly correct (within 0.3% of the constant-NN benchmark) but mode SHAPES that were badly, visibly wrong once plotted (values 100x too large in the interior) — because the boundary row’s coefficient came out exactly double its neighboring interior row’s reference to it, making the matrix asymmetric and numpy.linalg.eigh invalid on it (silently: eigh doesn’t check symmetry, it just uses the lower/upper triangle and returns garbage for the rest). The lesson generalizes: for any self-adjoint eigenvalue problem, checking eigenVALUES against a benchmark is not sufficient — check eigenVECTORS too.
