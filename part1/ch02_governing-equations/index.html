Ch 2. Governing equations & the Boussinesq approximation

Overview

The full equations of motion for a rotating, stratified fluid have a dozen terms. At any given scale, most of them are negligible — and this chapter’s entire content is the mechanical procedure for finding out which: nondimensionalize the Boussinesq equations by a length LL, velocity UU, and latitude, and the dimensionless coefficient in front of each term answers “does this term matter here?” directly. Every reduced equation set used later in the book (geostrophic balance, quasi-geostrophy, internal waves, Boussinesq convection) is what survives this procedure at that chapter’s particular scale.

Both fluids

  • Atmosphere: midlatitude synoptic weather sits at Ro∼0.1Ro\sim0.1 (geostrophically balanced); a single convective thunderstorm cell, at L∼10 L\sim10\,km, sits at Ro≳1Ro\gtrsim1 — genuinely different dynamical regimes governed by the identical Boussinesq equations, distinguished only by scale.
  • Ocean: mesoscale eddies sit close to Bu∼1Bu\sim1 (L∼LRL\sim L_R), which is exactly why they are the scale most efficiently generated by baroclinic instability (Ch. 16) — the regime map in Ch. 4 makes this connection explicit.
